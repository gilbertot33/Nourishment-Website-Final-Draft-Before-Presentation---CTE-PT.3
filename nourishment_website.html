Why the Bronx Needs Nourishment

Food insecurity affects thousands of Bronx families. Nourishment bridges that gap by turning unused rooftops, courtyards, and indoor spaces into productive, self-sustaining farms — managed by the very community they serve.

What Sets Us Apart

Technology Meets Community

Five pillars that make Nourishment's model uniquely effective and scalable.

🔬

Automated Technology

IoT sensors and AI monitor growing conditions and guide users through the mobile app, dramatically cutting training time and labor requirements.

⚡

Solar Independence

Photovoltaic panels paired with battery storage let each unit run completely off-grid on rooftops and courtyards, lowering energy costs.

🧩

Modular Design

Plug-and-play units link into clusters, allowing communities to grow from one pilot into a full neighborhood network without redesign.

📊

Predictive Analytics

AI forecasts crop yields, flags problems early, and helps partner organizations plan produce distribution to eliminate waste.

🤝

Community Ownership

Training programs, governance templates, and shared budgets transfer long-term operations to local stewards for lasting, independent impact.

🔒

Enterprise-Grade Security

Multi-factor authentication, encrypted cloud storage, and daily backups protect every site's operational and partner data.

From seed to table — how Nourishment gets fresh, locally grown produce to schools, food banks, and community kitchens across the Bronx.

Automated Harvest Monitoring

AI continuously analyzes sensor data (humidity, pH, temperature, light) to predict optimal harvest windows and alert site managers through the mobile app.

2

Volunteer Harvest Teams

Trained community volunteers and student tech teams receive in-app notifications and step-by-step guidance to harvest, sort, and package produce safely.

3

Partner Coordination

CRM tools track relationships with schools, nonprofits, and housing authorities. Delivery schedules are synced with partner kitchen calendars to minimize spoilage.

4

Yield Reporting & Analytics

Every delivery is logged with pounds grown, partner ID, and distribution date. Monthly reports are shared with funders and community governance committees.

5

Surplus & CSA Programs

Surplus produce beyond immediate partner needs is sold through Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) subscriptions, generating revenue for operational sustainability.

6

Waste Reduction

Predictive yield data from the AI model helps partners plan ahead, reducing over-ordering and ensuring every vegetable grown reaches a plate rather than a dumpster.

Marketing Strategy

How Nourishment recruits local champions, builds community trust, and drives measurable impact across the Bronx within 6 months of launch.

Strategic Goals

Launch Objectives

Outreach Tactics

  • Tenant and PTA outreach at targeted community meetings to identify and recruit local champions at each pilot site
  • Low-cost pilot installations in high-need census tracts to demonstrate real-world impact before broader rollout
  • Community planting days and STEM workshops at partner schools, generating hands-on engagement and earned media
  • Short-form video content and real-time yield updates shared via social media to build transparency and trust
  • Formal presentations to Bronx borough officials and city agency representatives for procurement conversations
  • Grant storytelling through data dashboards, volunteer hours logs, and cafeteria usage documentation

Funding & Revenue

Financial Model

Funding Sources

Grants, municipal programs, local sponsorships, in-kind school support, and small service subscriptions from partner organizations.

Revenue Streams

Surplus produce sales, CSA subscriptions, maintenance contracts with partners, and performance-based grants.

Sustainability Target

Cover routine maintenance costs within 18–24 months. Monthly financial reporting and one-year parts replacement reserve maintained.

Operating Costs

Maintenance, spare parts, coordinator stipends, cloud data hosting ($300/mo), app subscriptions ($250/mo), and community outreach.
